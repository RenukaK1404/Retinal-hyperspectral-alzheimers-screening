# Smartphone-Based Retinal Hyperspectral AI Framework for Alzheimer’s Risk Screening

## Overview

This repository presents the research framework and conceptual development of a smartphone-based retinal imaging and computational hyperspectral analysis system for Alzheimer’s-associated risk screening.

The project investigates the feasibility of identifying retinal spectral and microvascular biomarkers associated with neurodegenerative risk patterns using consumer-grade retinal fundus imaging combined with deep learning-based analysis.

The proposed framework integrates:

- Smartphone-based retinal image acquisition
- Computational RGB-to-hyperspectral reconstruction
- Retinal biomarker-oriented preprocessing
- Sequential deep learning analysis
- Mobile-compatible AI-assisted screening workflow

This repository focuses on the research direction, biomedical motivation, system-level architecture, literature synthesis, and conceptual framework of the project.

## Research Motivation

- Alzheimer’s disease (AD) remains one of the most challenging neurodegenerative disorders due to the difficulty of achieving affordable and accessible early-stage screening.

- Current diagnostic approaches such as PET imaging and cerebrospinal fluid biomarker analysis are expensive, invasive, and difficult to scale for population-level screening.

- The retina, as an extension of the central nervous system, offers potential for non-invasive biomarker detection. Recent retinal imaging studies suggest that wavelength-dependent retinal reflectance variations and microvascular alterations may correlate with amyloid-associated neurodegenerative changes.

- This project explores whether computational hyperspectral reconstruction from smartphone-acquired RGB retinal images can support scalable and low-cost AI-assisted retinal risk analysis.
